If \(n\) is not prime, there will be some \(i\) such that regions enclosed by \(i\) curves must be repeated to create a symmetric diagram, and thus we must have more than \(2^n\) regions in any symmetric diagram. Grünbaum gave the lower bound \(I(n)\) on the number of required regions:
\begin{equation*} I(n) = 2 + n (N_n - 2 ), \end{equation*}
where \(N_n\) is the number of binary necklaces of length \(n\text{.}\) The numbers \(N_n\) are well-studied, see [GKP, pp. 139-141]  for explicit formulae and further references; also see Subsection 4.5.2, Symmetric Diagrams, Necklaces, and Chains, for some important connections between necklaces and constructing symmetric Venn diagrams.
