The systematic mathematical study of symmetries of Venn diagrams on the sphere was initiated in Mark Westonâ€™s PhD thesis, [We09]. Some results of the thesis are reported in [RW11]. An involution is a self-inverse mapping. Amongst isometric mappings of the sphere onto itself, the involutory mappings fall into three categories: reflections about a plane through a great circle, reflections about a line through the center of the sphere, and reflections through the center of the sphere. We will refer to the symmetry of reflections through the center of the sphere as antipodal symmetry, since antipodal points are mapped to each other.
