The general construction of Edwards is monotone; the general construction of Venn is not. Examples of monotone and non-monotone Venn diagrams may be found on the Chapter 4, about symmetric Venn diagrams. The 3-Venn diagrams #3.12 and #3.13, shown in Figure 3.7.9, both in the same class, show that monotonicity is a property of Venn diagrams and not Venn classes, since #3.12 is monotone and #3.13 is not. In a monotone Venn diagram the \(k\)-regions, for any \(0 \lt k \lt n\text{,}\) form a “cycle”, in the sense that every \(k\)-region touches exactly two others, and they are connected together in a cycle under this relation [BR] .
