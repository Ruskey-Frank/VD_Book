Note that some authors refer to diagrams with fewer than \(2^n\) non-empty regions as Venn diagrams, but they are more properly termed Euler diagrams, after the mathematician Leonard Euler. More on these important diagrams can be found on the page on Generalizations and Extensions. We refer to a region enclosed by exactly \(k\) curves as a \(k\)-region or \(k\)-set.
