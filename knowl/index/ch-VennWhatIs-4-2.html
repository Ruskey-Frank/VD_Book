We follow Grünbaum [Gr75]  in first defining a more general concept, that of an independent family. Let \(\mathbf{C} = \{ C_1, C_2, \ldots, C_n \}\) be a collection of simple closed curves drawn in the plane. The collection \(\mathbf{C}\) is said to be an independent family if the region formed by the intersection of \(X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_n\) is nonempty, where each \(X_i\) is either \(int(C_i)\) (the interior of \(C_i\) ) or is \(ext(C_i)\) (the exterior of \(C_i\) ).
