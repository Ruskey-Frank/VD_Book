Let us count the number of vertices, edges, and faces in an \(n\)-Venn diagram. As an example, the three circle Venn diagram has 8 faces (corresponding to the 8 possible interior/exterior possibilities), 6 vertices (corresponding to the 6 intersections) and 12 edges. Recall Euler’s formula \(f = e - v + 2\) relating the number of faces, edges, and vertices of a graph embedded in the plane. Assuming that no three or more curves intersect at a common point (i.e., a simple Venn diagram), the graph is 4-regular and so we have \(e = 2v\text{.}\) By definition the number of faces is \(f = 2^n\text{.}\) It thus follows that \(v = 2^n - 2\) and \(e = 2^{n+1} - 4\) for simple Venn diagrams. By Observation 2.1.11 the maximum vertex degree is \(2n\)
