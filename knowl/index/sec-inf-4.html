Such a construction is possible for any \(n\text{,}\) and is attributed to Peter Shor in Knuth, Graham, and Patashnik [GKP,p.20,633] . An order-\(n\) (binary) de Bruijn cycle is a circular binary string of length \(2^n\) with the property that all \(2^n\) substrings of length \(n\) are distinct, and hence occur exactly once. For example, \(0011\) is a de Bruijn cycle for \(n=2\text{.}\) A wealth of information about de Bruijn cycles can be found in [Kn4] . The idea of Shor’s construction is to take a De Bruijn cycle \(C\) of length \(2^n\text{,}\) together with a regular \(2^n\)-gon inscribed in a circle. Make a curve by successively choosing a circular arc for each 1 in \(C\) and a polygonal edge segment for each 0. To obtain the other curves rotate this one about the central point by \(2 k \pi / 2^n\) radians for \(k = 1,2,\ldots,n-1\text{.}\) The example shown above is created by starting with the De Bruijn cycle \(C = 00011101\text{.}\)
