Such a construction is possible for any \(n\text{,}\) and is attributed to Peter Shor in Knuth, Graham, and Patashnik [GKP;p.20,633] . An order-\(n\) (binary) de Bruijn cycle is a circular binary string of length \(2^n\) with the property that all \(2^n\) substrings of length \(n\) are distinct, and hence occur exactly once. For example, \(0011\) is a de Bruijn cycle for \(n=2\text{.}\) A wealth of information about de Bruijn cycles can be found in [Kn4] .
